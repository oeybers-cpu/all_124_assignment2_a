Article Analysis

Analyze the argumentation strategies in your two selected articles

Analysis Guidance

Use these questions to analyze the argumentation strategies in your two selected articles. Consider how each author constructs their argument, what evidence they use, and how they attempt to persuade their audience.

Analytical Questions

1. What is the main argument or thesis of each article? How clearly is it stated, and where in the article does it appear?

2. What types of evidence does each author use to support their claims? (e.g., statistical data, case studies, theoretical frameworks, historical examples)

3. How do the authors establish their credibility or authority on the subject? Consider their use of specialized terminology, references to other research, or professional background.

4. What underlying assumptions or values shape each author's argument? Are these assumptions explicitly stated or implied?

5. How do the authors address counterarguments or alternative perspectives? Do they acknowledge and refute opposing views, or ignore them?

6. What persuasive techniques or rhetorical strategies does each author employ? (e.g., emotional appeals, logical reasoning, ethical arguments)

7. How is each article structured to maximize persuasiveness? Consider the organization of ideas, use of headings, and placement of key arguments.

Key Tips for Comparing Articles

  • Look for both similarities and differences in how the authors approach the same theme
  • Consider how the disciplinary background of each author might influence their approach
  • Evaluate which argument you find more persuasive and why
  • Note how each article contributes to a broader understanding of the theme

Critical Reading Reflection

Reflect on the articles in relation to your academic discipline and the South African context

Context

This section invites you to reflect on how the arguments in your selected articles connect to your academic discipline, your perspective as a student of Economics and Management Sciences, and the broader South African context.

Reflection Questions

1. How do the arguments in these articles connect to or challenge what you've learned in your EMS courses? What new perspectives do they offer?

2. How might the arguments in these articles inform potential solutions to economic or management challenges in South Africa? What practical applications can you identify?

3. Reflect on your position as a student of Economics and Management Sciences. How does your academic background shape how you interpret these articles?

4. Which of the two articles did you find more persuasive, and why? Consider the strength of evidence, clarity of argument, and relevance to the South African context.

5. What questions or areas for further investigation did these articles raise for you? How might you explore these questions in your future studies?

Session Reflection

Synthesize your analysis and reflection into key insights

1. What are the most significant similarities and differences between how your two selected articles approach your chosen theme?

2. How has engaging with these articles deepened your understanding of your chosen theme within the South African context?

3. What critical perspective will you bring to your reading reflection essay? What will be your main argument or thesis?

4. How will you synthesize the arguments from both articles to create a coherent critical response rather than just summarizing each article separately?
