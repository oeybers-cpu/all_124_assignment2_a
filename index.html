Once you have finished reading, proceed to "Part 1: Initial Thoughts" to begin your analysis.

Part 1: Initial Thoughts & Reactions

Read the provided text and capture your immediate reactions. Don't censor yourself; jot down whatever comes to mind.

  • What are your first impressions of the text?
  • What feelings or thoughts does it evoke?
  • Are there any parts that you immediately agree or disagree with?
  • What questions arise in your mind as you read?

Goal: To capture your unfiltered, initial engagement with the material before deeper analysis begins.

Part 2: Deconstruct & Analyze

Now, let's break down the text. Examine its components and how they fit together.

  • What is the author's main argument or thesis?
  • What evidence or examples does the author use to support their points?
  • What is the underlying structure of the text? (e.g., problem-solution, cause-effect)
  • Identify key terms and concepts. Are they clearly defined?

Goal: To understand the text on a structural and logical level.

Part 3: Critically Evaluate

Assess the strengths and weaknesses of the text. This is where you form your academic judgment.

  • Is the author's argument convincing? Why or why not?
  • Is the evidence provided sufficient, relevant, and credible?
  • Are there any biases, assumptions, or logical fallacies in the text?
  • How does this text compare to other works on the same subject?

Goal: To form a critical and informed opinion about the quality and validity of the text.

Part 4: Synthesize & Connect

Connect the text's ideas to your own knowledge and the broader context of your field.

  • How does this text relate to your own experiences or prior knowledge?
  • What are the broader implications of the author's argument for your field (EMS)?
  • How can you apply the insights from this text to your studies or professional practice?
  • What new questions or avenues for exploration has this text opened up for you?

Goal: To integrate the text's knowledge into your own intellectual framework and professional context.
