Identifying Argumentation Strategies

Authors use various strategies to persuade readers. Look for these techniques in your selected articles:

  • Ethos: Appeals to credibility and authority
  • Pathos: Appeals to emotions and values
  • Logos: Appeals to logic and reason
  • Evidence: Use of data, examples, and research
  • Rhetorical devices: Language choices that enhance persuasion
1. How does each author establish their credibility (ethos) to speak on this topic?
2. What emotional appeals (pathos) does each author use, and how effective are they?
3. Analyse the logical structure (logos) of each argument. How does each author build their case?
4. What specific evidence does each author present, and how credible is it?
5. How does each author address counterarguments or alternative perspectives?
6. Based on the youth unemployment article, what innovative approaches could South Africa adopt to address its youth unemployment crisis?
